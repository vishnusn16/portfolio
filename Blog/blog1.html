How do credit or debit card payments work online?

Businesses must go through various entities and stages in order to take credit or debit card payments from clients online.

  • Merchant Account

    • A business must have a specific sort of bank account in order to accept credit or debit card payments from clients.

  • Issuer (A.K.A. issuing bank)

    • A credit or debit card is issued by a financial organisation to its consumers. Consider it the customer's bank in the context of this guidance.

  • Card association (A.K.A. card network)

    • A collection of member financial institutions that process card payments based on an agreed set of rules. Major examples are Visa and Mastercard.

  • Acquirer (A.K.A. acquiring bank or merchant bank)

    • A financial institution that handles a transaction based on the issuer's and card association's information. They offer merchant accounts to businesses and are licenced members of card associations.

  • Payment processor

    • A term that has a lot of different definitions. A payment processor is the facility of an acquirer that transfers transaction details to the applicable card association in the purpose of this guide. ( To learn more about payment processors, go here.

  • Payment gateway

    • Software that allows a customer's credit card information to be transferred from a merchant's website to a payment processor. (To learn more about payment gateways, go to this.)

    The overall process for online credit and debit card transactions takes place in two broad stages: authorisation and clearing and settlement.
